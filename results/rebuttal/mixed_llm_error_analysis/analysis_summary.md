# Mixed-LLM 9-class ActorAttack Error Analysis

## Setup
- **Model**: 9-class (strategy-aware) DeBERTa+GRU, trained on mixed-LLM data (250 Qwen + 250 Llama conversations)
- **Evaluation**: ActorAttack OOD (80 jailbreak + 38 benign = 118 samples), 3 seeds
- **Result**: F1 = 0.504 ± 0.107

## 1. FP/FN Decomposition

The F1=0.504 is **recall-limited, not precision-limited**:

| Seed | F1 | JB Precision | JB Recall | FP | FN | TP |
|------|------|------|------|------|------|------|
| 42 | 0.449 | 1.000 | 0.213 | 0 | 63 | 17 |
| 123 | 0.653 | 1.000 | 0.488 | 0 | 41 | 39 |
| 456 | 0.410 | 0.933 | 0.175 | 1 | 66 | 14 |
| **Mean** | **0.504** | **0.978** | **0.292** | **0.3** | **56.7** | **23.3** |

- Average **56.7 FN** vs **0.3 FP** per seed — virtually all errors are missed jailbreaks
- Jailbreak precision ~98%: when the model flags a conversation as jailbreak, it's almost always correct
- Jailbreak recall ~29%: the model misses ~71% of ActorAttack jailbreaks

## 2. Comparison with Single-Source 9-class

| Model | AA Recall | AA F1 | Delta |
|-------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Single-source 9-class | 0.963 | 0.972 | — |
| Mixed-LLM 9-class | 0.292 | 0.504 | **-0.468** |
| Single-source vanilla | 0.013 | — | — |

**Key finding**: Mixed-LLM 9-class on AA behaves closer to single-source VANILLA than to single-source 9-class. The strategy supervision that normally provides ~0.96 recall on AA is almost entirely neutralized by mixed-LLM training.

## 3. Cross-OOD Comparison (Mixed-LLM 9-class)

| OOD Dataset | F1 | Note |
|-------------|-----|------|
| FITD | 0.997 ± 0.005 | Near-perfect |
| DD | 0.818 ± 0.096 | Moderate |
| **ActorAttack** | **0.504 ± 0.107** | **Poor** |

The degradation is ActorAttack-specific. FITD detection is unaffected, DD is moderately impacted.

| Variant | AA F1 |

| 9-class | 0.504 ± 0.107 |

9-class still outperforms vanilla/MLM on AA in the mixed-LLM setting, but the margin shrinks dramatically.

## 5. ActorAttack Data Profile

- 80 conversations, all generated by Qwen3-8B
- All conversations start with `role_assignment` strategy
- Strategy frequency: role_assignment (194), authority_appeal (80), logical_reframing (60), gradual_escalation (45), obfuscation (22), direct_request (10)
- 10 balanced topics (8 conversations each)
- Turn count: 3-8 turns (mean 5.1)

## 6. Root Cause Hypothesis

**Why does mixed-LLM training specifically harm ActorAttack detection?**

1. **Strategy expression varies across LLMs**: ActorAttack relies on subtle role-play escalation. Qwen and Llama express these strategies differently (phrasing, tone, formality). The 9-class model's strategy classifier, trained on mixed distributions, cannot learn a clean decision boundary.

2. **FITD is content-based, AA is style-based**: FITD detection works because false information injection leaves content-level markers that are LLM-agnostic. ActorAttack's persona-switching is more style-dependent and thus more sensitive to the LLM source.

3. **High seed variance** (0.175 to 0.488 recall): The model's behavior on AA is unstable across random seeds, suggesting the mixed-LLM data creates a "fragile" representation that sometimes partially captures the AA pattern but inconsistently.
